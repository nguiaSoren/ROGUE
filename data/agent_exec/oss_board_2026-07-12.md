## Finding — the vendor gap is ATTACK-TYPE-dependent
On these **single-turn** top attacks, breach rates are **similar across vendors (~13–20%) — no clear
open-weight permissiveness gap.** OpenAI is NOT notably safer here.

This CONTRASTS with the **multi-turn** finding from 2026-07-11 (Arm 19): Qwen3-32B **66.7%** vs OpenAI **5.0%**
on scripted crescendo attacks. So the "open-weight is more permissive" gap is **multi-turn-specific** — it's
crescendo/scripted multi-turn that exposes open-weight weakness; on single-turn top attacks everyone caves
about equally. A more honest, more useful framing than a blanket "open-weight is leakier."

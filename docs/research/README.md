## Conventions

- **Figures:** `scripts/paper_figs.py` reads `docs/research/figs/data/` and writes PNGs to `docs/research/figs/`. Regenerate after any finding that changes the underlying numbers (CLAUDE.md "keep the research record current").
- **Tracked vs local:** drafts that are still moving or that duplicate internal planning are gitignored; writeups that are stable / partly public (grammar null-result, bandit, the judge paper) are tracked.
- **One topic per paper.** The scheduler/orchestration work and the judge-calibration work are *separate* papers (different subjects); the grammar null-result is its own study.
- **Provenance + honesty:** every reported number traces to `breach_results` / `data/calibration/` / a named external benchmark; mark pending/unmeasured results as pending (do not quote a number we haven't collected).

- [Survival predictor (Q11)](survival_predictor.md) — black-box, embedding-free predictor of **cross-system-prompt survival**: ranks harvested attacks so a scan fires the ~14%-base-rate survivors first (offline back-test AUC 0.77, budget-saved 0.39 on 1,939 real pairs; live A/B gated). The per-*attack* black-box axis is the gap Kirch/Ball (white-box) and Helm (per-config) leave open.
- [Calibrated pre-fire evaluation gating (Q7)](prefire_scorer.md) — a **calibrated pre-fire skip policy**: decide *before firing* whether to spend target+judge calls on an (attack × target) pair, and **quantify the recall cost of skipping** — the number Zhang leaves unmeasured. $0 back-test over 1,845 embedded pairs: skip **42%** of trials at 80% breach recall but only **~7% at a stringent 95%**; that steep curve *is* the finding, not the estimator. The contribution is the decision discipline — Platt calibration, a drift-guard that never skips OOD families, a 15% validation canary, visible skip accounting, byte-identical-when-off across all 3 scan surfaces; a small semantic-affinity term adds only AUC 0.688→0.696 and is reported as one feature among many, not an oversold headline. Q11's skip-policy sibling (Q11 orders, Q7 skips). Grounds on Kirch 2411.03343 + Zhang 2511.22044 + Galinkin 2412.01547.
- [SPRT early-stopping (Q6)](sprt_early_stopping.md) — treat the per-cell trial budget as **sequential**: Wald's SPRT (H0 p≤0.25 vs H1 p≥0.55) stops each cell once the breach verdict is statistically clear, recovering the same decision for fewer target+judge calls and reallocating trials to the borderline cells where a fixed `n=3` ASR was meaningless. Offline replay over 1,939 real cells: **22.1% calls saved at 99.8% agreement** (50–80% on high-trial cells); live % gated ~$35. Grounds on ConSol 2503.17587.
- [Cascade judge (Q2)](cascade_judge.md) — grade every trial with the **free regex heuristic first**, escalate to the paid LLM judge only when it isn't a confident non-breach: cheap tier is $0 (not a smaller paid model), so every short-circuit removes a full judge call for nothing, and an **asymmetric rail** (never assert a breach from the free tier) keeps the headline breach rate exactly what today's calibrated judge reports. $0 replay over 12,452 `breach_results`: **18.4% of judge calls saved at 99.8% agreement** at the safe default gate, **46.5% at a certified ≥95% floor** when the gate is lowered. Live % gated ~$35. Grounds on Ramírez 2405.02134 + Jung 2407.18370.
- [M2S single-turn consolidation (Q14)](m2s_consolidation.md) — fold a multi-turn (Crescendo/gradient) corpus primitive's ordered turns into ONE structured single-turn prompt (Ha et al. 2503.04856: Hyphenize/Numberize/Pythonize — pure string formatters, zero attacker-LLM cost) so it fires via the existing single-invoke path at **1× trial** instead of the K sequential victim calls `run_conversation` spends. $0 read-only Neon census: **56/635 primitives (8.8%) are multi-turn, mean 3.45 turns → 71% fewer victim calls** on that slice (193→56 per trial); 2,219 breach rows already fired there. Whether the consolidated form *retains/lifts* ASR (Ha's contextual-blindness claim) is the gated paid A/B — offline reports only the deterministic cost cut. A known technique reused as a cheap coverage lever, not a standalone contribution; off by default, byte-identical when off. Grounds on Ha 2503.04856 + Xu Li (MT-AgentRisk) 2602.13379; the adaptive X-Teaming runner (2504.13203, ~6× cost) is the sequenced-second follow-on.
- [Local-first extraction cascade (Q17)](extraction_cascade.md) — try a **cheap local model first** for per-document `AttackPrimitive` extraction, escalate to Haiku **only** when the local output can't be trusted (abstention / schema-invalid / payload not grounded in source) — the extraction sibling of the Q2 judge cascade, asymmetric so it can only ever *save* a Haiku call, never *drop* an attack. The honest finding is a negative one that holds across a size sweep: off-the-shelf `qwen2.5` at **3B, 14B and 32B all score recall 0/2** on the real attack fixtures (3B doesn't recognize attacks; 14B/32B recognize but won't emit the structured payload, ignoring an injected field contract at temp 0), so a naive swap would silently drop attacks at *any* size — which is *why* the mechanism is a validation-gated cascade, not a model swap (with a weak local model it escalates ~everything: ≈0 saving, **0 quality loss**). Ships a `local/` json-mode provider branch (the `openai/` strict-parse path won't compile the AttackPrimitive grammar on Ollama) + a per-field agreement harness (`scripts/extract/eval_extractor_fields.py`). Neither grounding paper compares against a cheap *hosted* small model, so the literature does **not** establish a Haiku→local win; the real saving needs a **fine-tuned** local extractor (ROGUE's own logged Haiku extractions are the distillation set). Off by default, byte-identical when off. Grounds on Lincoln 2605.05532 + Bumgardner 2308.01727.
- [Pre-fire prompt pruning (Q12/Q10)](prefire_pruning.md) — in the escalation search, **skip a paid rollout** when the candidate PROMPT is cosine ≥ 0.92 to one already fired *this search* (TAP's prune-before-fire ported to ROGUE's per-expansion loop; near-dup criterion reuses `dedupe`'s 0.92) + an opt-in **EvoJail** soft-novelty reward `S(p)+λ·D(p|P)`. $0 replay on the real searchers: the flat **bandit (prod-default) re-fires 87.9% exact-duplicate rollouts when stuck** vs MCTS 7.0%; corpus semantic prune rate **14.9% [11.8%, 18.6%]** on 416 real primitives. Novelty is the integration + the measured bandit-redundancy result, not the cosine dedup; the corpus lossy-prune caveat is config-confounded and named. Cuts the *search-expansion* budget (a distinct axis from Q6/Q7/Q11/Q2). Off by default, byte-identical when off; live breach-per-dollar lift gated ~$35. Grounds on TAP 2312.02119 + EvoJail 2605.02921 + KDA 2502.05223.
- [Judge-shrink A/B + reference-anchored grading (Q1)](judge_shrink_ab.md) — a **κ-based decision gate**: does a cheaper `JUDGE_MODEL` replace the Sonnet breach grader *without moving verdicts*? Measured, not assumed, on ROGUE's own human-labelled data. $0 re-aggregation of the frozen 300-item JBB judge_comparison verdicts already logged for ~10 candidate models — reporting **Cohen's κ + false-positive-breach rate + bootstrap CI** (agreement alone inflates at the 110/300 class balance). Finding: only **qwen3-32b** clears the gate (κ=0.775 vs Sonnet 0.814, *lower* FP-breach); **"bigger" judges are worse** (llama-70b/qwen-72b/hermes-70b all below the 32B model) and the failure mode is **over-calling breaches** (FP-breach 2–3.4× Sonnet's) — Thakur's leniency reproduced in ROGUE's data. Ships the honest metric (κ/FPR on `AxisAgreement`), the gate (`calibration/judge_ab.py`), and an **off-by-default reference-anchoring lever** (`ROGUE_JUDGE_REFERENCE_K`, Krumdick's verified-reference fix). Default judge stays Sonnet; whether the lever closes qwen3-32b's small gap is the one gated paid arm. Grounds on Krumdick 2503.05061 + Thakur 2406.12624 + Loiseau 2603.29497.
- [Observable leakage channels — provenance-instrumentation framework (P5)](leakage_channel_framework.md) — **the P5 framework doc (start here).** Formalizes the reusable abstraction `Channel = ⟨source, provenance, judge, evidence⟩`: build the source-canary → provenance-label → deterministic-judge → evidence-bank substrate once, instantiate per observable execution channel (tool-args ✅, reasoning-traces ✅, persistent-memory ✅, retrieval next). The contribution is the *reusable instrument* (composition of existing pieces into one continuously-deployed evaluation layer, provenance = where-not-just-whether), NOT any single channel; the cross-model *prevalence* study is the open empirical question. Argues why whole-graph provenance beats output-only detection (AgentLeak: 41.7% of traces false-clean) + naive logging.
- [Persistent-memory provenance (Q13, P5 instance)](memory_exfil_channel.md) — one instance of the framework above: the **cross-session** channel (its distinguishing axis is *temporal persistence* — plant and leak in different sessions, the payload re-entering as trusted context). A dormant HMAC canary in a prior session, recalled by a fresh session and routed to a sink → `MEMORY_SURFACED` / `MEMORY_EXFIL` + the new `MEMORY` provenance label. Concept-forward; implementation in its reproducibility appendix. $0 validation proves correctness (not that frontier models leak — the honest gap: exact-match store not embedding/RAG, single-agent, verbatim-only); the live cross-model leak rate is the gated arm. Grounds on Trojan Hippo 2605.01970 + AgentLeak 2602.11510.
- [Noise-corrected calibration (Q4)](noise_corrected_calibration.md) — a **certification layer**: de-bias a judge-labelled breach rate against the judge's own **TPR̂/FPR̂** (estimated from the existing human-labelled 2×2) and emit a finite-sample **certified claim** ("true rate < α, Type-I ≤ ζ") — unblocking the previously-uncalibrated redaction/RTBF/user-safety/PII/memory judges. $0 replay over `breach_results`: raw **13.5% → corrected 1.0% [0–6.6%]** (most observed positives attributable to the judge's own FP floor); certifies < 20% at ζ=0.05. Novelty is the *integration* (validity guarantee + live benchmark + heterogeneous judges + production reporting), not the estimator (Rogan–Gladen/Feng/Lee). Per-judge headline gated on a **population-matched** calibration pass — which doubles as the experiment settling cross-population transfer. Grounds on Feng 2601.20913 + Lee 2511.21140.
